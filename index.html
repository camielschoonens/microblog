Leadership

Jun 16, 2023

This quote from a now-departed and respected Jewish teacher is powerful, because… Many of us belong to communities that are based on the consolidation of power and #control by those who claim to “lead” the community. These aren’t true leaders. Leadership isn’t about keeping power or control: it’s about giving it to others. This is why the practice of improvisation is so important: we practice giving to others, not taking or controlling for ourselves.

If you belong to a community where the “leader” doesn’t actually understand true leadership, trust your instincts. Find a different community that’s aligned with your humanity, ethics, values and morals. And if you can’t find one: start one. Others will join you because you are never alone. In fact, you might be the very spark that others need to experience in order to shift. Give gifts and then don’t stop giving.

Introduction

My journey in life started 1978 in the south of the Netherlands. I don’t know a lot about my time in that region of the country anymore as we moved around quite a lot as family in the ‘80’s and ‘90’s. Together with my parents and two siblings, we lived in several different places in the Netherlands and in the USA.

My experience in the USA as a young teenager formed me more in life then I would have expected at the time it happened. I’m certain that it’s one of the main reasons of why I like working in international environments today.

Today I’m living in the lovely town of Oudewater. I’m living here since 2006 with my wife and family and it’s the place I always love returning too from any travel that I do.

Converting W to kWh in Home Assistant

May 21, 2023

I’ve been measuring my solar panel production in Home Assistant ever since the panels were installed and I really like the insights that this has been giving me on a daily basis.

Home Assistant has a really nice build-in energy dashboard in which I was able to capture the total produced power of all my solar panels combined by default. This in itself already gave good insights on the daily production but I wanted to refine the insights a bit more as I have two groups of solar panels, one group facing east, one group facing west. You can imagine that depending on the day and season the production on the east and west side of the house differs.

When going through the Home Assistant sensors that my inverter ‘SMA Solar’ provides I noticed that there are actually Watt (W) sensors available for each of the panel groups. These sensors just weren’t showing up as source data for the energy dashboard.

After some reading on the internet I figured out that the reason that I can’t add these sensors to the default energy dashboard is because it expects kilowatt (kWh) sensors.
